Opportunity

The Equilibrium is an undergraduate research journal that was recently accepted to obtain an official UC San Diego domain. As a result, I had the opportunity to redesign the website to improve usability and increase the journal's online presence. The original website was hosted with Google Sites, which meant customization difficult and design was limited to specific themes. I utilized HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to rebuild and redesign the journal's website.

Personas

In order to include all of the journal's target users, I created personas to identify each group's goals, need, and attitude. These personas helped guide my designs to ensure they addressed user needs.

Alex is a first year college student who is looking for a way to get involved in an on campus organization. He has been looking at a lot of student organization websites and is pretty tired of needing to search for the information he wants. Alex wants to know what the organization does, how he can get involved, and who he can ask for more information.

Erika is a graduating senior who finished her department honors thesis project and is looking for a way to get it published. She is a student in the visual arts department, and all of the publishing avenues on campus are for science majors. Ultimately, she just wants to know what she can do to get her honors thesis published.

Devon is a professor who likes to keep up with student opportunities to pass on to her students. Her schedule is almost always jammed packed, so she doesn't have much time to read entire websites. She needs a website that gives her the quick facts but is still a resource that she can give her students.

Wireframes

I used Sketch to create low-fidelity website wireframes of the website to visualize layout, information placement, and outline the information architecture.

Homepage

The homepage was the biggest opportunity to improve branding and usability because it is the first page that visitors see when they go on the website. The old website homepage had minimal information as to what The Equilibrium was. The only content was an events calendar that was empty for more than 90% of the time, which meant that the rest of the page was empty. In the redesign, the visible section of the page is a maroon geometric background in order to create a stronger brand image. Additionally, I included more information about the journal on the homepage: what the journal is, what it does, and how to get involved with the journal.

Application Pages

These secondary pages needed to be reorganized because submitting a manuscript to publish is different from applying to join the journal's editorial board. Thus, I separated out the original website's "Application" page to a "Submit" page for authors to learn how to submit their research for consideration and a "Apply" page for students to learn how to joining the organization.

Publication Pages

Another major redeisgn was the publication pages. The original page only had links to the online readers. However, there was a lot of new information to include. Additionally, in order to increase our online presence, each individual publication is accessible on the website as well.

Reflection

Redesigning an entire website (even if it's a site that only has five pages) took a lot of time. But, I found that I really enjoyed coding and creating a website from scratch. It gave me ample opportunity to practice the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills I already had. At the same time, this experience forced me to dig deeper. I had to really understand the nuances of the coding languages I was using, to find what the best practices are, and to discover how to implement specific designs in order to make a website that was aesthetic and functional. This process was a practice of trial, error, and Googling, which has, ultimately, made me a better designer.
