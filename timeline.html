1. Academic Research in the Comparative Cognition Laboratory

In Spring 2016, I joined a research lab on campus within the Cognitive Science Department. The lab studied human (and primate) cultural and social norms by using ethnography, study designs, and surveys. This lab started my interest in learning more about how humans sense and perceive the world and interact with the people and things around them.

I also started working on the lab website -- my first experience designing and developing!

2. Print Design at The Equilibrium

In Winter 2017, I joined the editorial board of The Eqiulibrium, an undergraduate reserach journal, as a design editor. The journal had a weak brand presence and lots of inconsistencies, leaving an unprofessional look. So, this was an opportunity to improve branding, visual appearances, and make the pages more reader-friendly.

3. Design Coursework

Throughout the 2017-2018 school year, I was taking several design courses. Some classes I took were: Usability & Information Architecture, Interaction Design Startup, Interaction Design, Social Computing, and Cognitive Design Studio.

My favorite class was the Interaction Design course because I got to experience the full design and development cycle of a web app. I was the core developer for the app, working with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (Node). After we finished, the team was able to use the app for their own living spaces because it is hosted on GitHub.

Another fun class was the Interaction Design Startup class. We were tasked with creating and designing an experience that could potentially be a startup idea. We started with a topic area, travel, and then conducted research and ideated. After we chose an idea, we paper prototyped and created mid and high fidelity prototypes.

4. Web Development for The Equilibrium

From June 2018 to October 2018, I worked on a personal project to update the website for The Equilibrium. The journal had relatively weak online presence, so it was an opportunity to not only reach more people, but also serve as a resource for people to access when they need information about the journal.

5. UX Internship at the Birch Aquarium at Scripps

During Summer 2018, I worked as a UX Designer for the Birch Aquarium at Scripps. In a team of 10, we worked to create a floor-ready, multisensory, and interactive exhibit that taught guests about waves. The UX Team generated 5 user flow maps and conducted over 60 rounds of prototype user testing over the course of 10 weeks. The exhibit is now on display at the aquarium for guests to experience!

6. UX Designer & Web Developer at the WTS at UCSD

Starting Summer of 2018 and throughout the 2018-2019 school year, I worked as a UX Designer and a Web Developer for the Workplace Technology Services at UC San Diego. I worked on campus client websites, designing and creating full websites, and I fulfilled ad hoc design requests as well.

I also helped conduct user research and synthesis on a new app experience for a new campus building. With the data from a series of 1:1 semi-structured interviews I conducted, other designers and developers are working on the application ideation and design now.

7. Office Hours Experience Honors Research Project

During the 2018-2019 school year, I worked on an honors research project. I utilized a mixed-methods study design that investigated what factors affect undergraduate office hours attendance rates. The results unveiled common painpoints and opportunities for interventions to improve overall experience.
